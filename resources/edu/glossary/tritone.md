A **tritone** spans three whole tones — half an octave. Historically called the
*diabolus in musica*, it is the most unstable interval in tonal music. It sits
between the third and seventh of a dominant chord, and its urge to resolve
inward (or outward) is what drives a V chord toward its tonic.
