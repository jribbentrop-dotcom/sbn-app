Every note has two properties: its **pitch** and its **duration**. Duration is measured relative to the bar. A **whole note** fills an entire 4/4 bar; a **half note** fills half; a **quarter note** one beat; an **eighth note** half a beat.

Two modifiers extend a note's length without changing its pitch:

- A **dot** adds half the note's own value. A dotted quarter lasts 1½ beats — indispensable in Bossa Nova, where the clave pattern lives almost entirely in dotted rhythms.
- A **tie** connects two notes of any value into a single sustained sound. It's the only way to hold a note across a barline, and the engine of syncopation — attacking off the beat and sustaining through the next strong beat.

Reading durations fluently is the prerequisite for internalising any rhythmic pattern. Once the values are automatic, you stop counting and start feeling the groove.
