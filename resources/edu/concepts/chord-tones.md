Every chord is built by stacking thirds above a root. The first four notes — root, third, fifth, seventh — are the **chord tones**. Notes stacked beyond the seventh are **extensions**: the 9th, 11th, and 13th.

<sbn-widget slug="chord-tones" />

Extensions add colour without changing the chord's harmonic function. A Cmaj9 is still a Tonic chord — the 9th just gives it more shimmer. In Jazz and Bossa Nova, plain triads are rare; extended voicings are the default vocabulary.

The third and seventh are the most load-bearing tones — they define the chord's quality (major, minor, dominant). The fifth is often omitted in drop voicings because it adds little colour. Extensions are the last to arrive and the first to be cut when the voicing gets crowded.

Understanding which tones are essential and which are decorative is what lets you build practical guitar voicings from a chord symbol.
