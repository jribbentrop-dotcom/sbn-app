Every note shape carries a duration — a fixed relationship to the beat. Reading rhythm means instantly recognising these shapes and knowing how they fit inside a bar.

<sbn-widget slug="note-durations" />

The relationships are always halving: a **whole note** lasts four beats; a **half note** lasts two; a **quarter note** one beat; an **eighth** half a beat; a **sixteenth** a quarter of a beat. Each step down doubles the density.

In notation the shapes are systematic: open note head with no stem = whole; open head with stem = half; filled head with stem = quarter; filled head with one flag or beam = eighth; two flags or beams = sixteenth.

**For Jazz and Bossa Nova**, the eighth note is the primary rhythmic unit. The swing feel is an uneven eighth — long-short, approximating the first and third notes of an eighth-note triplet. Bossa Nova rhythm patterns on guitar are built from interleaved sixteenths and eighths, implying the clave without stating it explicitly.
