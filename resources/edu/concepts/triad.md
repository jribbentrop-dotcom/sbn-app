The **triad** is the foundation of Western harmony. Stack two thirds and you
get three notes: the *root*, the *third*, and the *fifth*.

The quality of the two thirds decides the chord's character:

- A **major triad** has a major third on the bottom and a minor third on top —
  bright and stable.
- A **minor triad** flips them: a minor third below, a major third above —
  darker, but equally settled.
- A **diminished triad** stacks two minor thirds, and an **augmented triad**
  stacks two major thirds — both symmetrical and restless.

Every richer chord — sevenths, ninths, extensions — is a triad with more
thirds piled on top.
