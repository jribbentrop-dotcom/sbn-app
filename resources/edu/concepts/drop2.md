A **closed voicing** packs all four notes of a 7th chord inside a single
octave. On guitar that means four notes crammed onto adjacent strings — often
unplayable.

The **drop 2** fix: take the *2nd-highest* voice and drop it down an octave.

<sbn-widget slug="drop2-visualizer" quality="dom7" />

The voicing now spreads across a wider range. On guitar this opens the grip up
so it falls naturally under the hand — which is why drop-2 shapes are the
backbone of jazz comping.

The note content is unchanged — same four pitches, same chord. Only the
*spacing* moved. That single move turns an awkward cluster into a practical,
resonant voicing.
