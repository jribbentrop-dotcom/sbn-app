**Voice leading** is the art of moving from one chord to the next while each
individual voice travels as little as possible. Smooth voice leading is what
makes a progression sound connected rather than like a series of unrelated
shapes.

The core principles:

- **Keep common tones.** If two chords share a note, hold it in the same voice.
- **Move stepwise.** When a voice must change, move it by a step rather than a
  leap.
- **Resolve tendency tones.** Leading tones rise; sevenths fall.

Good voice leading is why drop voicings and shell voicings exist — they put the
notes where the hand can move them the least.
