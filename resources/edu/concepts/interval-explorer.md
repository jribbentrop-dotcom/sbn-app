An **interval** is the distance between two pitches, measured in semitones. Every chord, scale, and melodic phrase is built from intervals — understanding them is understanding the raw material of music.

<sbn-widget slug="interval-explorer" />

Twelve intervals span an octave, each with a distinct character:

- **Unisons and octaves** — the same note. Perfect consonance.
- **Fifths and fourths** — open and powerful. The frame of most harmony.
- **Thirds and sixths** — the warm core of chords. Major thirds are bright; minor thirds are darker.
- **Seconds and sevenths** — close and tense. The seventh chord extensions that give Jazz its colour.
- **The tritone** — exactly half an octave. Maximally unstable, the engine of dominant tension.

In Jazz and Bossa Nova, the intervals that matter most are the major third (defines major quality), the minor seventh (defines dominant and minor 7th chords), and the tritone (the tension inside every V7 chord that resolves to the I).
