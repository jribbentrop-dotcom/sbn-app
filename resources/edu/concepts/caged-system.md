The guitar fretboard can feel like a wall of dots. The **CAGED system** reveals its hidden structure: every major chord can be played in five distinct positions, each named after the open-chord shape it derives from — C, A, G, E, and D.

The shapes tile the neck in a fixed order. Once you find the root, the pattern always continues the same way: C→A→G→E→D→C again, repeating every octave. You are never more than two or three frets from a usable position.

The practical payoff is two things:

- **Fretboard navigation.** When a Bossa Nova comping pattern sits in one position, you can shift the same chord voicing up the neck without losing your place harmonically.
- **Shape relationships.** Each CAGED position contains the same triad — root, third, fifth — just redistributed across different strings. Understanding where those notes land is the foundation for adding extensions and colour tones in any position.

Jazz and Bossa guitar often favours the inner shapes (A, G, E) for their drop-voicing possibilities, but knowing all five means you always have a choice.
