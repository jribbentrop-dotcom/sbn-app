Once you have a 7th chord, the stacking doesn't stop. Add another third above the 7th and you get the **9th**; another above that gives you the **11th**; one more yields the **13th**. These are the **extensions** — tones that add colour without changing the chord's harmonic function.

<sbn-widget slug="chord-extensions" />

Each of the five 7th-chord qualities has its own natural extension ceiling:

- **maj7** tops out at the #11 (Lydian colour — a raised 4th avoids the dissonant clash with the major 3rd).
- **dom7** reaches the 13th and uniquely admits **option tones** — ♭9, #9, #11, and ♭13 — which intensify the tension before resolution.
- **m7** and **m7♭5** extend naturally to the 11th; the minor 3rd and 11th coexist without friction.
- **dim7** is so harmonically dense and symmetrical that extensions beyond the 9th are rarely used.

The third and seventh are load-bearing — they define quality. The fifth is often omitted in real voicings. Extensions are the last to arrive and the first to be dropped when a voicing gets crowded.
